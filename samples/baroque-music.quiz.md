## Q2 · True or False: George Frideric Handel was born in England.

```quiz
id: q2-handel-birthplace
points: 1
difficulty: easy
```

- [ ] True
- [x] False

> Handel was born on 23 February 1685 in Halle, Germany (the same year as Bach). He moved to London in 1712 and became a British subject in 1727. His oratorio "Messiah" (1741) premiered in Dublin, not London.

> Basso continuo (a continuous bass line with improvised harmonies), terraced dynamics (abrupt changes rather than gradual crescendos), ornamentation and counterpoint are hallmarks of the Baroque style. Serialism is 20th-century; minimalism is post-1960.

## Q4 · Fill in the blank: The first opera ever composed, "L'___", was written by Claudio Monteverdi in 1607.

```quiz
id: q4-monteverdi-opera
points: 2
difficulty: hard
hint: "It's the name of a figure from Greek mythology who descended into the underworld"
bloom: remember
```

**Answer:** Orfeo

> "L'Orfeo" (1607) by Claudio Monteverdi is widely considered the first fully realised opera. It tells the myth of Orpheus and Eurydice and was performed at the Gonzaga court in Mantua.

## Q7 · True or False: The harpsichord can produce gradual dynamic variations (soft to loud) by varying key pressure, just like a piano.

```quiz
id: q7-harpsichord-dynamics
points: 2
difficulty: medium
bloom: understand
tags: [instruments, harpsichord]
```

- [ ] True
- [x] False

> Unlike a piano, a harpsichord plucks its strings with a quill (plectrum) regardless of touch pressure — the mechanism is binary. This is precisely why Baroque composers used "terraced dynamics" (sudden shifts between loud and soft) rather than gradual crescendos.

## Q8 · Fill in the blank: Bach's set of 30 keyboard variations commissioned by Count Keyserlingk are known as the ___ Variations.

```quiz
id: q8-goldberg
points: 2
difficulty: medium
hint: "Named after the harpsichordist who may have performed them"
```

**Answer:** Goldberg

> The Goldberg Variations (BWV 988, 1741) are named after Johann Gottlieb Goldberg, harpsichordist to Count Keyserlingk. The story that they were written to help the count sleep may be legend, but the work stands as one of the supreme achievements in keyboard music.
